Key Insights Discovered (8 insights)

  • Customer count is the strongest revenue driver (r=0.74, 57% importance in ML models)
  • Average Order Value is the second strongest driver (r=0.54, 36% importance)
  • Marketing spend has a moderate positive effect (r=0.25, each $1 adds ~$1.52 revenue)
  • Operating hours, employee count, and foot traffic have NO statistically significant impact on revenue
  • Only 3 out of 6 factors are statistically significant (p<0.05)
  • The interaction of Customers × AOV explains 95.4% of revenue variance
  • Random Forest model achieves best prediction accuracy (R²=0.949, CV R²=0.946)
  • Revenue distribution is right-skewed (skew=0.61) — more low-revenue days than high

Strategic Recommendations for the CEO

  • Focus on increasing customer footfall — each additional customer adds ~$5.54 to daily revenue
  • Implement upselling strategies to raise Average Order Value — each $1 increase adds ~$243 daily
  • Maintain marketing spend as it shows positive ROI ($1.52 return per $1 spent)
  • Operating hours optimization is NOT a revenue lever — consider reducing to cut costs
  • Staffing levels do not significantly impact revenue — optimize for service quality instead
  • Location foot traffic alone does not drive sales — conversion rate matters more than walk-bys
